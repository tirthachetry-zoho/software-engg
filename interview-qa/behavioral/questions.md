# Behavioral Interview — Interview Q&A

## 1. What is the STAR method?
**Answer:** **S**ituation (context), **T**ask (your responsibility), **A**ction (what *you* did), **R**esult (outcome, quantified). Always focus on *your* contribution, not the team's, and quantify impact (%, ms, $ saved, users).

## 2. Tell me about a time you showed leadership.
**Answer:** Use STAR: led a migration/incident, drove a design decision, or mentored juniors. Example: "I noticed flaky deploys (S); I owned fixing CI (T); I introduced contract tests + staged rollout (A); deploy failures dropped 70% (R)."

## 3. Describe a difficult production incident you handled.
**Answer:** Show calm, structured debugging: detected via alerting, mitigated (rollback/circuit breaker), root-caused (heap dump/thread dump), and prevented recurrence (monitoring/runbook). Emphasize communication during the incident (status updates).

## 4. Tell me about a conflict and how you resolved it.
**Answer:** Pick a real technical disagreement (e.g., monolith vs microservices). Show you listened, presented data/tradeoffs, found common ground, and reached a decision respecting both sides. Avoid blaming individuals.

## 5. Describe a failure and what you learned.
**Answer:** Choose a genuine, non-fatal failure (a bug shipped, a wrong architecture call). Focus on the **lesson** and the **systemic fix** (process, tests, review) — not just "I was careful after." Demonstrates growth.

## 6. Tell me about a time you mentored someone.
**Answer:** Describe identifying a gap, pairing/code-review guidance, and the mentee's growth. Shows investment in team scaling — a key SDE3 expectation (you raise others, not just yourself).

## 7. How do you handle conflicting priorities / stakeholder pressure?
**Answer:** Show structured prioritization (impact vs urgency), transparent communication of tradeoffs, and data-driven negotiation. Example: pushed back on an unrealistic deadline with a phased delivery plan that met the critical path.

## 8. Describe a project where you drove the architecture.
**Answer:** Highlight *why* you chose the design (scale, team, cost), the tradeoffs you accepted, and how you brought others along (RFC, design review). SDE3 = you design and drive, not just implement.

## 9. Tell me about a time you disagreed with your manager.
**Answer:** Show respect + data: presented your concern with evidence, proposed an alternative, and either convinced them or committed fully once decided. Demonstrates senior maturity (disagree-and-commit).

## 10. How do you ensure code quality on your team?
**Answer:** Concrete practices: PR review standards, CI gates (tests/lint/coverage), design docs for big changes, and leading by example. Show you *raised the bar* for others, not just wrote good code yourself.

## 11. Describe a time you had to make a tradeoff decision.
**Answer:** Pick a real one (consistency vs latency, speed vs perfection, build vs buy). Show you articulated the options, made a call with incomplete info, and owned the outcome. Senior = comfortable with tradeoffs.

## 12. Why should we hire you for an SDE3 role?
**Answer:** Tie to the bar: deep technical depth (Java/JVM/scale), ownership of outcomes (not tasks), ability to drive design and mentor, and clear communication. Back each claim with a brief STAR example from your history.
