# Resume Preparation — Interview Q&A

## 1. What should an SDE3 resume demonstrate?
**Answer:** **Scale** (QPS, data volume, users), **performance/cost wins** (latency −42%, $ saved), **leadership** (drove design, mentored), **architecture ownership**, and **metrics**. Use bullets that show *impact*, not just responsibilities.

## 2. How do you quantify achievements on a resume?
**Answer:** Attach numbers to every win: "Reduced p99 latency **42%** serving **18M req/day**", "Cut infra cost **$30K/mo** via autoscaling", "Led migration of **12 services** with **0 downtime**". Quantified impact is what recruiters/hirers scan for.

## 3. What is the "XYZ" resume bullet formula?
**Answer:** **Accomplished [X] as measured by [Y], by doing [Z].** Example: "Improved deploy reliability (X) by 70% (Y) by introducing contract tests + canary rollouts (Z)." Forces every bullet to show action + metric.

## 4. How long should an SDE3 resume be?
**Answer:** **1–2 pages** max. Senior resumes favor depth over length — recent/relevant roles detailed, older ones summarized. Don't pad; every line should earn its place with impact or scope.

## 5. What should you leave OFF the resume?
**Answer:** Irrelevant early jobs, every tech you've touched (list what matters to the role), personal details, and vague claims without proof. Avoid "responsible for" — use "owned/drove/delivered". No objectives; use a sharp summary.

## 6. How do you present a leadership/ownership story?
**Answer:** Show you *drove* outcomes: "I identified the scaling bottleneck, proposed the CQRS split, aligned 3 teams, and delivered it." Highlight decisions made, tradeoffs accepted, and people influenced — not just code written.

## 7. How do you tailor a resume to a backend/SDE3 role?
**Answer:** Emphasize Java/JVM depth, distributed systems, microservices, and scale. Mirror the JD's keywords (Kafka, K8s, Spring) where truthful. Put the most relevant, impressive work first (recruiters skim top third).

## 8. What is a strong resume summary for SDE3?
**Answer:** 2–3 lines: years of experience, core strength, and a headline achievement. E.g., "8+ yrs building scalable Java backend systems; led design of a payment platform processing 20M txns/day; cut p99 by 40%."

## 9. How do you handle a gap or job-hopping?
**Answer:** Be honest and brief; frame gaps as learning/contract/health without over-explaining. For job-hopping, show a trajectory of increasing scope/impact. In interviews, focus on *why the next role fits* your growth.

## 10. Should you include GitHub/projects/open-source?
**Answer:** Yes if relevant — a strong GitHub, OSS contributions, or a notable side project signals depth and passion. Link them. For SDE3, system-design depth in past roles matters more than hobby projects, but both help.

## 11. How do you show system-design experience on a resume?
**Answer:** Name the systems and their scale: "Designed a multi-region notification platform (Kafka + Redis) handling 5M events/min with 99.95% SLA." Concrete architecture + metrics prove design capability better than "worked on microservices."

## 12. What common resume mistakes kill SDE3 chances?
**Answer:** Listing responsibilities instead of impact, no metrics, buzzword-stuffing without proof, too long, and weak summaries. At SDE3 the bar is *driving outcomes* — your resume must show you've already operated at that level.
